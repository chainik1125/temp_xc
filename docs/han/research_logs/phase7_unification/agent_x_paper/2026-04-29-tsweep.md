> Phase 7 paper artefact (ii). Both T-sweep families at every-T cell with
> two seeds (1, 42); FLIP-corrected; S=32 mean-pool aggregation.
> **Task set: PAPER** (paper headline; 16 of 36 SAEBench tasks across
> balanced clusters; rationale in `2026-04-29-paper-task-set.md`).

### Data

- 16 tasks (PAPER reduction of the 36-task standard set).
- S = 32 left-aligned cache, mean-pool aggregation.
- Seed=1 + seed=42; per-cell `n_seeds=2` for every entry below.
- Per-cell metric: cross-seed mean of per-task means.

### Shape and headline takeaways (PAPER)

- **Barebones TXCDR**: peak at T=4-7, gradual decline through T=18-20,
  partial recovery at T=24 / T=32. At k=5 best-T = 7 (0.8674); at k=20
  best-T = 3 (0.9095). T=32 stays close to T=24 — the dramatic high-T
  collapse seen on certain individual tasks (e.g., `europarl_fr`
  drops from 1.0 to 0.8) is averaged out by the cluster-balanced set.
- **Hill-climbed H8 multidistance** (T ≤ 9): peak at T=4 for both
  k_feat values (0.8757 / 0.9084), slightly above barebones.
  σ_seeds 0.001-0.008 — small.
- At k_feat=5 the hill-climbed family is consistently 0.005-0.01 AUC
  above barebones at the same T.
- At k_feat=20 the families overlap closely (within σ_seeds).
- **`winogrande` is the single major positive-T outlier** but is
  averaged out in the family-mean curves; the per-task plot
  (`2026-04-29-per-task-tsweep.md`) shows it monotonically climbing
  from 0.62 at T=3 to 0.90 at T=32 — the cleanest empirical
  validation of TXC's structural inductive bias.

### Cross-task-set sanity check

Comparing best-T cells across full-36 vs BALANCED vs PAPER
(k_feat=5):

| family | full-36 best-T mean | BALANCED best-T mean | PAPER best-T mean |
|---|---|---|---|
| barebones | 0.8952 (T=7) | 0.8607 (T=4) | 0.8674 (T=7) |
| hill-climbed H8 | 0.8957 (T=4) | 0.8641 (T=8) | 0.8757 (T=4) |

Different absolute AUCs (PAPER excludes some near-ceiling tasks)
but qualitatively the same shape (peak at small T, decline at high T).
