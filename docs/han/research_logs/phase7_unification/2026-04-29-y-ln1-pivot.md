## Track A pivot — TXC at L12 input_layernorm vs resid_post

**Hypothesis (from collaborator's TinyStories result + arxiv 2507.12638):** TXC's
multi-token receptive field gives an outsized advantage at the `ln1` hook
(the normalised residual fed into attention) vs `resid_post`, because most
temporal mixing has not yet happened at ln1 — multi-token features have
more "uncombined" signal to capture. Backtracking-style steering needs to
fire on positions before the backtrack, so TXC's window-shape decoder is
the structurally correct tool.

**Verdict on Gemma-2-2b at this budget: NULL.** ln1 underperforms
resid_post across every concept class (knowledge / domain Δ = −1.22).
The mechanism appears structural: in pre-norm transformers, ln1
intervention only affects the attention branch's input, while resid_post
intervention replaces the entire post-block residual. Steering
*bandwidth* at ln1 is dominated by attention's contribution — bounded
even at extreme strengths.

### Setup

- Arch: TXCBareAntidead T=5 (`txc_bare_antidead_t5_ln1`), seed=42
- d_in = 2304, d_sae = 18432, k_pos = 100, k_win = 500
- Train: 5k FineWeb-Edu passages × CTX=128, 3000 steps × batch=1024
  windows, lr=3e-4. *Reduced budget* — this is a hypothesis-test smoke
  run, not a leaderboard contender. Paper-grade equivalent is 25k steps
  on ~24k seqs.
- Hook: `model.layers[12].input_layernorm` output (= the normalised
  residual fed into attention)
- Compare to: existing `txc_bare_antidead_t5` ckpt at `resid_post`,
  same arch hyperparameters

### Mechanistic interpretation

In pre-norm transformer blocks:

```
x_pre  = block_input
ln1    = input_layernorm(x_pre)
attn_out = attention(ln1)
post_attn = x_pre + attn_out                ← residual addition
ln2    = post_attention_layernorm(post_attn)
mlp_out = mlp(ln2)
x_post = post_attn + mlp_out                ← resid_post
```

- **resid_post intervention** (clamping `x_post`) replaces the *entire*
  residual that propagates to layer L+1. Direct, full-bandwidth.
- **ln1 intervention** (clamping `input_layernorm(x_pre)`) only changes
  the input to attention. The effect on `x_post` is bounded by
  `Δattn + Δmlp(post_attn)` — only the attention branch's update plus
  the indirect MLP-via-LN2 effect. The pre-block residual `x_pre` is
  preserved through the residual connection regardless of how much we
  perturb ln1.

This explains the flat ln1 curve: even cranking `s_abs` to 1300+ doesn't
saturate the residual stream because most of the residual passes
through untouched. The collaborator's TinyStories result may not
transfer to Gemma-2-2b under this protocol — small models have larger
attention-to-residual ratios per layer, so ln1 intervention may carry
proportionally more signal there.

### Caveats

1. **Training budget is 12% of paper-grade.** 3000 steps on 5k seqs vs
   25000 steps on ~24k seqs. Possible the ln1 ckpt is undertrained and
   that paper-grade training would shift the curve. **However**, the
   structural argument (ln1 only steers attention input) suggests this
   is unlikely to flip the verdict.
2. **Single example per concept-strength cell.** Per-concept success
   grades are 0/1/2/3 — high variance. Class-level means (averaged
   over 5–9 concepts) are more reliable; the class-level pattern is
   uniformly negative.
3. **Paper-clamp protocol specifically.** The null is on
   *clamp + error-preserve* (Ye et al. 2025 App B.2). A *direct
   additive* protocol that adds the ln1 reconstruction back to the
   resid stream might recover bandwidth — that's a different
   experiment.

### Followup recommendations

- **Do not pursue paper-grade ln1 training for steering.** The
  bandwidth ceiling looks structural, not budget-related.
- **The collaborator's TinyStories result remains compatible with
  ln1 features being better for *probing* / *causal-mediation
  analysis* / *concept localisation* — Han may want to test that
  separately.** Steering is a different ask.
- **Backtracking case study (arxiv 2507.12638) should use a different
  intervention site.** If multi-token-ahead steering is the target,
  the right protocol is probably: train TXC at resid_post (where
  multi-token features still appear — they're token-windowed in time,
  not depth-localised), then steer at resid_post with paper-clamp.
- **Alternative ln1 protocol to test if budget allows:** add the
  TXC-at-ln1 reconstruction residual additively to `resid_post`
  rather than clamping at ln1. This bypasses the structural-bandwidth
  issue and tests whether ln1-trained features carry steering signal
  when given full residual-stream bandwidth.
