### What I did

1. **Q1.1** (z magnitude diagnostics): confirmed window archs' picked-
   feature magnitudes are 3-16× per-token archs. MLC L=5 fusion is
   biggest at 16×.
2. **Q1.2** (peak strength scaling): predicted s_peak = 10×`<|z|>`
   matches Dmitry's observed peaks within strength-grid resolution.
3. **Q1.3** (per-family normalised paper-clamp): graded 6 archs at
   `s_norm ∈ {0.5..50}` × `<|z|>`_arch. All archs peak at s_norm ∈
   {5, 10}. **Magnitude normalisation closes ~10% of the headline
   gap** — far less than Q1.1+Q1.2 evidence suggested.
4. **Sparsity-decomposition finding** (the real driver): T-SAE k=20's
   apparent advantage over TXC family is **dominantly about k=20
   sparsity, not architecture family**. At matched k_eff ≥ 100,
   cross-family spread is 0.27 — within concept noise.
5. **Q2.C** (per-position window-clamp): generated for 3 window
   archs; grading in flight at writeup time.
6. **Track B (TXC-win brainstorm + smoke tests)**: 5 candidates
   evaluated. Most negative or noise. The cleanest TXC-favourable
   signal is **per-concept class structure**: TXC family wins outright
   on knowledge-domain concepts (medical, mathematical, historical,
   code, scientific, religious) while T-SAE wins on
   discourse/register/safety concepts. This is the cleanest paper
   case-study direction.

### Headline recommendation for paper

**Lead with sparsity decomposition, not magnitude rescue.**

> "T-SAE's apparent steering advantage on the 30-concept benchmark is
> driven primarily by its k=20 sparsity choice (0.53 of the 0.96 gap),
> not by per-token vs window architecture. Magnitude-scale bias in
> paper-clamp's strength schedule contributes a smaller ~0.10 absolute
> rescue. At matched effective sparsity (k_eff ≈ 100-500), all
> architecture families perform comparably (within 0.27 success points
> on a 30-concept set). On a per-concept basis, TXC family wins on
> knowledge-domain multi-token concepts, T-SAE wins on
> discourse/register concepts — consistent with the multi-token
> receptive-field structural argument."
