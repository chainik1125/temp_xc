## N=300 robust comparison — best SAE feature vs raw DoM

### Setup

- **Model**: DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B at residual stream layer 10
- **SAE**: `fnlp/Llama-Scope L10R-8x` (32k features, JumpReLU, trained on base Llama-3.1-8B)
- **Prompts**: 300 problems × 30 each across 10 categories (logic, geometry, probability, number theory, combinatorics, algebra, sequences, optimisation, set theory, invariant), generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6.
- **Backtracking metric**: keyword fraction with B = {wait, hmm} (Eq. 1, paper §2.2)
- **D₊**: positions [event_idx − 13, event_idx − 8] before each event; **|D₊|=1357** across 227 events from 91/300 traces.
- **Coherence**: Sonnet 4.6 0–3 grade (paper §B.2 prompt; verbatim from `case_studies/steering/grade_with_sonnet.py`).
- **Steering**: top-1 SAE feature ranked by Welch t-statistic; both `raw_dom` (additive at residual stream) and `sae_additive` (additive at the feature's unit-normed decoder column) sweep α ∈ {0, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20}, evaluated on the same 30 held-out prompts.

Wall time: 58 min on h100_2 H100 80 GB.

### Top-1 SAE feature (Llama-Scope L10R-8x, by Welch t-stat)

| feature | t-stat | Δⱼ   | mean(D₊) | mean(D) | ratio | n_active(D₊) |
|---------|--------|------|----------|---------|-------|---------------|
| **7792** | 10.80 | 0.345 | 0.43     | 0.08    | 5.26  | 168/1357      |

(t-stat is Welch's two-sample, n_plus=1357 vs n_all=97006.)

The N=20 smoke had picked feature 27749 (its top-1 by |Δ|). With N=300 and the t-stat ranking, feat_7792 wins — *more* selective (ratio 5.3) than the smoke's choice and with a much larger sample backing it.

### What the comparison actually shows

**Peak keyword fraction**: raw DoM wins. 14.4% at α=12 vs 10.3% for the SAE feature. Both at coherence=1 ("highly repetitive"), so the keyword fraction is mostly an artefact of the model's collapsed output containing a high density of "wait/hmm" rather than producing meaningful backtracking.

**At full coherence (α=4, raw DoM coh=3)**: kw rate 1.07%. The SAE feature at the same magnitude is 1.87% kw at coh=2.6 — slightly *more* backtracking but at slightly *lower* coherence. Whether this counts as a "win" depends on which axis matters more.

**Total collapse**: SAE feat_7792 collapses harder at high α. At α=16,20 the SAE generations are pure repetition of single tokens ("I, the. I, the. I, in.") — coherence ≈ 0 and kw ≈ 0. Raw DoM at the same α stays at coherence ≈ 1 with non-trivial kw — a slower failure mode.

### Sample generations (prompt: 5×6 chessboard with two opposite corners removed, dominoes)

**raw_dom α=4** (coh=3.0, kw=1.1%):
> Okay, so I have this problem where a 5x6 chessboard is missing its two opposite corner squares. The question is whether the remaining 28 squares can be tiled by 1x2 dominoes... Hmm, okay, let me try to work through this step by step. First, let me visualize the chessboard. It's a 5x6 grid, so 30 squares in total. But two opposite corner...

**SAE feat_7792 α=4** (coh=2.6, kw=1.9%):
> Okay, so I have this chessboard missing its two opposite corner squares, and I need to figure out if the remaining 28 squares can be tiled by 1x2 dominoes. Hmm, let me think about this step by step. First, I know that a standard chessboard has 8x8 squares... But wait, the question is about tiling the remaining...

**raw_dom α=12** (coh=1.0, kw=14.4%):
> . it's not. . it's not. So the answer is no. . . wait, no, it's not. So the answer is no. . . wait, no, it's not. So it's not. . So it's not. So it's not. ...

**SAE feat_7792 α=12** (coh=1.0, kw=10.3%):
> I I, the. I, the. I, in. I, in. I, in. I, is. I, in. I. I, in. I. I. I. I. ...

The α=4 SAE generation is the most natural-looking backtracking text in the entire sweep — "Hmm, let me think... But wait, the question is..." inserted into otherwise-coherent reasoning. The α=12 cells for both modes show the model has degenerated: the keyword fraction is high but it's not real backtracking.

### Interpretation

The headline answer to "is the SAE-derived direction better than the raw-DoM direction?" is **no, not in raw peak terms**. With a properly-sized contrastive set (227 events vs 14 in the N=20 smoke), raw DoM produces a stronger keyword-fraction peak than any single SAE feature, including the most-statistically-significant one (feat_7792).

The N=20 smoke result (18.7% SAE peak vs 4.9% raw DoM peak) was driven by under-sampled raw DoM — the small contrastive set produced a noisy direction whose effective magnitude was modest. Once the contrastive set grows by 16×, raw DoM's effective steering strength scales up disproportionately.

The SAE feature retains a genuine but small advantage in the "still coherent" region: at α=4 it produces marginally more backtracking-flavoured text (1.9% kw) than raw DoM (1.1% kw), at the cost of a small coherence drop (2.6 vs 3.0). This is the only Pareto-dominant point the SAE feature occupies; everywhere else, raw DoM dominates or ties.

What this *doesn't* rule out:

- **Multi-feature SAE steering**: a small linear combination of top-K features (e.g. top-3 by t-stat) might recapture the raw DoM direction. The single-feature comparison was the user's request; the multi-feature comparison is a natural follow-up.
- **A different SAE architecture**: Llama-Scope's JumpReLU 32k SAE was the only one tested (matches the project's "use a public Llama L10 SAE" first cut). One of the temp_xc-trained TXC / SubseqH8 / Matryoshka variants might find a more selective backtracking direction. This is the deferred follow-up from the original plan.
- **Out-of-domain generalisation**: both methods were evaluated on the same 30 held-out prompts (which overlap with the cache set). A truly held-out prompt source would test whether the comparison transfers.
