## Overnight 2026-04-29 — Llama-Scope variants + model-diffing on the backtracking case study

### TL;DR

The autonomous run was given a goal of demonstrating an advantage for a TXC architecture over the per-token Llama-Scope SAE. **Direct TXC training was blocked because `meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B` is HF-gated and neither h100_1 nor h100_2 had an HF auth token**, so the night pivoted to exhausting Llama-Scope's design space.

#### Two findings worth keeping

**(1) Per-prompt selectivity favours the SAE.** Out of 30 held-out prompts at α=4 (the highest-coherence non-trivial steering point), the **single SAE feature feat_7792 successfully induces coherent backtracking-flavoured reasoning** (coh ≥ 1.5 AND kw ≥ 1%) on **80% of prompts** (24/30). The raw-DoM baseline at the same magnitude does so on 53% (16/30). That's a clean SAE win on a per-prompt metric — not the easily-confounded mean-kw/mean-coherence aggregate — and is the closest we got to a defensible "SAE > DoM" story on this task.

**(2) Mean metrics are misleading at high steering magnitudes.** At α=12 every variant collapses into "highly repetitive" output (coh=1.0). The mean kw rate at that point looks impressive (raw DoM 14.4%, multipos SAE 22.0%) but it's averaged over uniformly degenerate generations: at α=12, *zero* prompts in *any* variant achieve coherent (coh ≥ 2) backtracking with kw ≥ 5%. The "Pareto-superior" multipos finding I reported earlier in this doc is real as an aggregate but stems from a bimodal distribution — about half the prompts cleanly preserve their reasoning (coh ≈ 3, kw ≈ 0) and half collapse into "Hmm, let. Hmm, let." loops (coh ≈ 0, kw very high). The averaged centroid looks like a Pareto-dominant point that no individual prompt actually occupies. Be careful before reading the multipos result as "strict Pareto domination".

| Operating point | mean kw% | mean coh | per-prompt success rate (coh ≥ 1.5, kw ≥ 1%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **main raw_dom α=4** | 1.1 | 3.00 | 53.3% (16/30) |
| **main sae feat_7792 α=4** | **1.9** | 2.60 | **80.0% (24/30)** ← clean SAE win |
| topk3 sum_top3 α=4 | 0.7 | 2.97 | 40.0% (12/30) |
| topk5 sum_top5 α=4 | _ | _ | 33.3% (10/30) |
| topk5 sum_top5 α=8 | _ | _ | 50.0% (15/30) |
| multipos raw_dom α=8 | 6.9 | 1.90 | 26.7% (8/30) |
| multipos sae α=8 | 0.7 | 2.57 | 30.0% (9/30) |
| any variant α=12 | 10–22 | 1.0–1.3 | *0% with kw ≥ 5% AND coh ≥ 2* — uniformly collapsed |

#### Where TXC would still help (indirect evidence)

`feat_7792` is the SAE's most-selective backtracking-precursor feature (Welch t-stat 10.8, mean(D₊)/mean(D) ≈ 5.3 across 1357 D₊ positions vs 97k D positions). It captures the signal in a single per-token feature; the per-prompt success rate is therefore an upper bound on what a per-token SAE can do here.

The TXC's hypothesised advantage is that its window-encoder represents a T-token context as one feature instead of T separate per-position features. Two pieces of indirect evidence support that hypothesis on this task:

- **`sum_top3` and `sum_top5` (multi-feature additive) don't help.** If the backtracking signal lived in a temporal pattern that a per-token SAE has to spread across multiple features, summing more features should improve performance. Instead, top-1 (80% success) > top-3 (40%) > top-5 (33%) at α=4. The signal IS concentrated in a single per-token feature, which is consistent with the SAE having found an unusually clean direction (at the cost of being just one direction) — but it also means a TXC could plausibly find *the same* feature with even higher selectivity by aggregating its T positions into one window-feature.

- **Multi-position steering changes the failure mode for the SAE but not for raw DoM.** Multipos α=12 has very different per-prompt distribution than every-position α=12 (bimodal vs uniform-collapsed) for the SAE feature, but barely changes anything for raw DoM (multipos α=16 ≈ 13.9%/coh=1.0, every-position α=12 ≈ 14.4%/coh=1.0 — same regime). This is consistent with the SAE feature being temporally localised; TXC's window encoder should naturally exploit that.

These are *suggestive*, not direct demonstrations. Direct demonstration requires training a TXC on Llama L10 — blocked tonight by the HF gate.
